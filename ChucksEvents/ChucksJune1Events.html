Double Workshop: Invasive Pest Management and Noxious Weed Control

Cortez Historic Preservation Walking Tours

Cortez, CO – The City of Cortez Historic Preservation Board will host free historical walking tours on Saturday, June 6.
Two guided tours will be available:

  • Downtown District Walking Tour: A stroll along Main Street exploring the businesses and events that shaped Cortez’s early commercial development.
  • Historic Montezuma Avenue Walking Tour: A look at the history and stories behind the homes along Montezuma Avenue.

Both tours last between one hour to 90 minutes.
Registration is required, but participation is free. Interested participants should reserve a ticket ahead of time via the links below or calling the Montezuma Heritage Museum at (970) 516 -1100. Eventbrite registration link: https://www.eventbrite.com/o/121076208969

A suggested donation of $5 will benefit the Montezuma Heritage Museum and may be provided at the time of the tour.

  • Saturday, June 6

o            Downtown District Tour: 10:00 AM
o            Montezuma Avenue Tour: 1:00 PM

All tours begin at the Montezuma Heritage Museum at 35 South Chestnut Street.
Participants are reminded to wear comfortable shoes, sun protection and bring a bottle of water and your curiosity.

For more information on the events for Historic Preservation Days, please visit: https://www.cortezco.gov/909/Historic-Preservation-Days
 
Indigenous Poetry Reading at the Sunflower

Join us for an evening of powerful poetry and storytelling with local Indigenous poets Kirbie Bennett, Eirene Hamilton, and Esther Belin on Saturday, June 6th at the Sunflower Theater from 7-8:30pm.

In celebration of the theme “A Place We Call Home,” these three acclaimed Diné poets will share original work exploring identity, land, language, memory, and belonging. No matter your familiarity with poetry, this evening offers an opportunity to hear deeply rooted, moving, and inspiring voices from our region.
Tickets are sliding scale $15-$25, and can be found here: https://sunflowertheatre.org/product/8347/

Yoga of Awareness and Transformation

My heart is humble and my spirits excited First class next Sunday - 7th June at Mancos Public Library

My homemade Chai will be flowing in abundance and sweet sweet Gong Vibrations will support your savasana!!

Traditional Kundalini yoga, also known as the Yoga of Awareness and Transformation, combines breathing techniques, movement and meditation to provide profound physical and mental benefits.
Whether it’s your first time doing yoga or your 100th… it is easy to learn and can even be done while sitting in a chair!

Some of the key benefits of this practice include reduced stress and anxiety, increased energy levels, improved spinal flexibility, strengthened nervous system, enhanced emotional balance and greater mental clarity.

We are currently amid highly stressful and chaotic times which are calling upon our own inner peace and grounding.
Practicing Kundalini yoga in your community and groups help us to tap into the Revolutionary Love these times are calling for. Love for ourselves, love for our families, love for our community, love for humanity and love for our planet.

These classes are inspired by the books Sage Warrior by Valarie Kaur and Fierce Vulnerability by Kazu Haga which both speak to us about finding the wisdom to envision a new world and the courage to fight for it.

Through self-care practices we can once again tap into love and joy living life in a brilliant, compassionate and powerfully hopeful way.
